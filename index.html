Building a Federated Repository and Increasing Access through Cybertraining

Project

Cybertraining materials abound, but they can be difficult to find, and often have little information about the quality or relevance of offerings. Leveraging existing software technology, the HPC-ED Pilot project team will build a platform that enables the community to more effectively share and discover training materials through a federated repository.

Key features of this platform include:

  • Organizations and authors will retain physical and legal ownership of their materials, sharing only catalog information.
  • Organizations can expand their local portals by incorporating the best and most appropriate materials from both local and remote sources.
  • Learners will benefit from materials that have been reviewed and described more clearly.
  • Learners and organizations will be able to quickly and accurately find materials via standardized descriptive tagging.

The HPC-ED Pilot project will demonstrate that resource providers, campus portals, schools, and other organizations can seamlessly integrate training materials from multiple sources into their own familiar interfaces and publish their local training materials to a much wider audience.
